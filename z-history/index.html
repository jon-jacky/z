Z History

Annotated links to sources (by others, not me) that document significant moments in the history of the Z notation. They trace the growth, then decline, of interest in Z.

Approaches to proof in Z by Andrew Martin (1997): "Various attempts at supporting proof in Z are described in the literature. This paper presents a survey of these approaches, and the underlying semantic issues which make proof in Z a non-trivial task. ... Special care is given to an account of the peculiarities of Z schemas. ... The broad conclusion is that none of these approaches is a clear winner at present ..."

This thorough report surveys the work of much of the Z community at the time of its greatest expansion. It explains some difficult technical issues in Z better than any other source I know.

I think this report also shows why interest in Z declined from about this time, though criticism of the community is never made explicit here. The Z community encountered technical issues that were at first poorly understood, which turned out to be more difficult than expected, and could no longer be avoided. By then it was too late to adopt the obvious solution of banishing the problems by simplifying the notation. Each group -- there were many -- tried to cope with the difficulties in its own way. No consensus solution emerged. The community was fragmented. The larger world began to abandon Z.

Jon Jacky, revised Oct 2022
